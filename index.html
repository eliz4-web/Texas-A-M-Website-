This website is about Pennies on Sully at Texas A&M University. This tradition at Texas A&M University honors Lawrence Sullivan "Sul" Ross, former president of the college (1891–1898) known for saving the school. Students place pennies on his statue in Academic Plaza for good luck on exams, referencing a legend that he would accept "a penny for your thoughts" for tutoring help. Our group is implementing a new tradition that honors past Revellies who have served as the university's First Lady, recognizing their contributions to campus life and traditions. For more information about the current tradition, please visit an external website.

Every student has felt the pressure of exams and tests, and placing a penny on Sully's statue is a way to seek good luck and guidance. Reveille will be honored at the Reveille Shrine. It serves as a memorial to the university's past Revellies who have served as the university's First Lady.
